PEPRSONALITY

  My name is Dalton Weir and I am a diversely trained digital professional currently living in Edinburgh, Scotland.
  I was born and raised in Buffalo, NY – a medium sized, former “rust-belt” city in the Great Lakes region of the American northeast. As an immigrant city, Buffalo has been influenced by a conflagration of world traditions and cultures that shaped the city’s rich corporate and cultural tapestry. This combination of old-world traditions and new-world economic and cultural development has shaped much of who I am professionally and personally.
  In the nooks and crannies of that Pan-American labyrinth, I encountered a fusion of human delicacy and urban design. In Buffalo, you regularly pass by large surrealist murals and complicated graffiti adorning the faces of aged neoclassical buildings constructed at the turn of the century. My time spent sneaking through the run-down grain mills of ‘Silo City’ or riding my bike across town at 12 am on Sunday nights with 500 other adventurers taught me to notice the subtle nuances of connecting with others. When I moved to university, I carried those memories with me into my studies of literature, social culture, and digital spaces to find I was happiest when working with others to drive forward a future of inclusive technology. The cutting edge of new digital business provides space and resources for this ethos to flourish.
  Now, I am living and working in the UK – my chosen home – with teams and individuals that see this need and are working to drive the industry forward. As I continue my work, I hope to continue connecting with others in valuable, exciting ways and welcome those who wish to reach out regarding opportunities. For now, here is a portfolio of work – The Campaign of Me.
  My public and professional persona is marked by a desire to improve the experiences of those who interact with the projects I work on. I truly believe that the smallest positive impact on someone’s experience can be significant. An accessible PDF here or well-designed product there could be the difference between a person exiting in frustration or discovering something special. The latter is what I want. More importantly, I hope it’s what we all want as digital professionals. If you feel the same, feel free to reach out and chat!
  I read articles by companies all the time about how they’re ‘rewriting the manual on [insert project here]’ or ‘changing the world with [insert solution here]’. That’s excellent if it works out, and I applaud them wholeheartedly for their incredible work. But often, I also see time, manpower, and funds disappear into these projects – resources that could impact user experience massively if applied to making products and solutions more inclusive. I feel that the small details matter because they show respect and care for the customer.
  In my work, I’ve committed myself to learning as much as I can about how accessible design impacts people’s experiences of products; then I observe these impacts through data collection and analysis. This information is then used to inform each project thereafter. Multidisciplinary teams and working styles are becoming more popular for a reason. Customers’ experiences reflect their fluid and diverse personalities, so it is important to connect with those personalities as best we can to maximise that experience.
  Check out my three CV versions below for more technical detail on my past positions!
  Outside of work, I love to explore digital spaces – to travel the proverbial world of online interaction. Whether it be gaming, reading blogs, creating digital art, or teaching myself to code, I find myself learning constantly from the incredible people and products online. I suppose you could say I am a bit of a futurist. I love discovering the ways in which people today interact with and discuss the ideas and questions of tomorrow. If you ever want to talk about robots, just let me know!
  I’m also a big film buff. One of my favourite films is The Fifth Element starring Chris Tucker and Bruce Willis, and I’m also a very big Moulin Rouge fan. I’m a bit of a sucker for theatrical interpretations of modern pop and rock. As for music, I love R&B, smooth alt-rock, electro swing, house, and classic jazz.
  Hang on there. I don’t want you thinking that I only watch movies and sit on my computer! I’m quite and active person and love playing post-work footy when I’m not at rugby practice. I also enjoy a night out with friends and coworkers and am always up for heading out for a coffee or lunch to get to know new friends and acquaintances better. Fancy some good coffee and a better chat? Just let me know!
